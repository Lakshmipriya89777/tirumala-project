About The Temple : The Akasaganga is one of the most sacred and famous theerthams (holy water places) in Tirumala. It is a beautiful natural waterfall located about 3 kilometers away from the main Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple. The name “Akasaganga” means “Ganga from the sky,” and devotees strongly believe that this water is not ordinary but has a divine and heavenly origin. Pilgrims consider it a very holy spot because it is closely connected to the spiritual traditions of Tirumala and Lord Venkateswara.
According to ancient legends, Akasaganga was revealed through the deep devotion of a great devotee named Tirumala Nambi. In olden days, he used to bring water from Papavinasanam for the daily rituals and abhishekam of Lord Srinivasa. One day, during his journey, a hunter asked him for water. After the water was finished, the hunter shot an arrow into the rocks, and miraculously a fresh stream of water started flowing from the hill. Tirumala Nambi realized that the hunter was actually Lord Srinivasa in disguise, blessing the devotees with this sacred water source. Thus, Akasaganga became an important holy waterfall of Tirumala.
Historically, the water from Akasaganga was carried manually by priests and used for the abhishekam (holy bath) of Lord Venkateswara. Even today, it remains spiritually significant, and the temple administration continues to respect its sacred connection. Many devotees believe that praying near Akasaganga or taking blessings from its water helps in removing sins, bringing purity, and granting good fortune.
Akasaganga is also associated with Anjana Devi, the mother of Lord Hanuman. It is believed that she performed penance in this area, and nearby there is a small temple dedicated to Sri Balanjaneya Swamy. The place is surrounded by green hills, rocky paths, and the soothing sound of flowing water, creating a peaceful environment for devotees and visitors.
Overall, Akasaganga is not only a holy pilgrimage site but also a serene natural treasure of Tirumala, reminding devotees of divine grace, devotion, and spiritual purity. Peaceful natural surroundings, best visited in the morning. Entry is free for all pilgrims

Typical visiting hours: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM daily — this is the commonly cited schedule for devotees to visit and pray near the waterfall/theertham during the day.
