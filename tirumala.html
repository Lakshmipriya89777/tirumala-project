About The Temple : Sri Venkateswara Temple in Tirumala is not just a temple. It is like a living place of devotion where prayers, rituals, and service happen every minute of the day. Lakhs of devotees visit daily, and everything works in an organized and disciplined way. Sri Venkateswara Temple, also called Tirumala Balaji Temple, is one of the most famous and holy temples in India. It is located on the Tirumala hills near Tirupati in Andhra Pradesh. The temple is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, a form of Lord Vishnu. Long ago, it is believed that Lord Vishnu came to earth during Kali Yuga to help people and protect them from suffering. He chose Tirumala hills as his home and lived there as Lord Venkateswara. The hills are green, peaceful, and full of divine energy, which makes them very special. Before staying in Tirumala, Lord Venkateswara asked permission from Lord Varaha, who was already living there. Lord Varaha happily allowed him to stay. Because of this, devotees believe that visiting Varaha Swami first and then Balaji is the correct and traditional way of worship. The temple has a long history and has been protected and developed by many kings, especially the Vijayanagara kings. They donated gold, jewels, land, and wealth to the temple. Even today, millions of devotees donate money and offerings with faith and love. People believe that Lord Venkateswara fulfills wishes and gives health, wealth, peace, and happiness. Devotees often offer hair (tonsure), money, laddus, and prayers to show gratitude. The famous Tirupati Laddu is given as prasadam and is loved by devotees all over the world. Every day, lakhs of people visit the temple for darshan. Important festivals like Brahmotsavam, Vaikuntha Ekadashi, and Rathasaptami are celebrated in a grand way. The temple rituals are done with great care and devotion. In short, Tirumala Balaji Temple is a place of strong faith and hope. People believe that Lord Venkateswara listens to prayers and blesses everyone equally. Visiting this temple gives peace to the mind and strength to the heart.
Brahmotsavam – The Grand Festival
Brahmotsavam is the biggest and most important festival in Tirumala. It usually lasts for 9 days. During the Tirumala Brahmotsavam (annual festival), Lord Venkateswara (Sri Malayappa Swamy) is taken out in a procession on several vahanas (divine vehicles) over the nine days. Each day has its own special vahana or procession vehicle that has its own meaning and tradition. “The vahana schedule may vary slightly each year according to TTD announcements.”
Day 1 – Dwajarohanam (Flag Hoisting)
The festival begins with Dwajarohanam, the hoisting of the Garuda flag. It is a message that the Lord’s divine celebrations have started. Devotees gather in large numbers for the first day rituals.
Day 2 – Morning: Pedda Sesha Vahanam
Lord rides on the Great Serpent Adisesha. It represents protection and divine power.
Evening: Chinna Sesha Vahanam
Lord again comes on the smaller serpent vehicle. It shows the Lord’s care towards devotees.
Day 3 – Morning: Hamsa Vahanam
Lord rides on the Swan vehicle. Swan symbolizes purity and wisdom.
Evening: Simha Vahanam
Lord rides on the Lion vehicle. Lion represents courage and victory over evil.
Day 4 –Morning: Kalpavriksha Vahanam
Lord rides on the divine wish-fulfilling tree. Devotees believe wishes will be fulfilled.
Evening: Sarvabhoopala Vahanam
Lord appears as the king of all worlds. It shows He is the ruler and protector of everyone.
Day 5 –Morning: Mohini Avataram
Lord takes the form of Mohini, the divine enchantress. This represents the Lord’s power to protect goodness.
Evening: Garuda Vahanam (Garuda Seva)
This is the most important day. Lord rides on Garuda, His divine eagle. Lakhs of devotees come for this special darshan.
Day 6 –Morning: Hanumantha Vahanam
Lord rides on Hanuman’s vehicle. It symbolizes devotion, strength, and service.
Evening: Gaja Vahanam
Lord rides on the Elephant vehicle. Elephant represents royalty and stability.
Day 7 –Morning: Surya Prabha Vahanam
Lord rides on the Sun vehicle. Sun represents brightness and energy.
Evening: Chandra Prabha Vahanam
Lord rides on the Moon vehicle. Moon symbolizes calmness and peace.
Day 8 –Morning: Rathotsavam (Chariot Festival)
Lord is taken in a huge wooden chariot. Devotees pull the chariot with devotion.
Evening: Ashwa Vahanam
Lord rides on the Horse vehicle. Horse represents speed and protection of devotees.
Day 9 –Morning: Chakrasnanam
The Sudarshana Chakra is given a holy bath in Swami Pushkarini. Devotees take a sacred dip believing it removes sins.
Evening: Dwajavarohanam
The festival ends with lowering the temple flag. Brahmotsavam concludes peacefully. Each day of Brahmotsavam is special because Lord Venkateswara blesses devotees in different divine forms. The festival shows Tirumala’s rich culture, devotion, and spiritual power.
